Singapore Green Plan 2030

The Singapore Green Plan 2030 is a whole-of-nation movement to advance Singapore's national agenda on sustainable development. This plan charts ambitious and concrete targets over the next 10 years, strengthening Singapore’s commitments under the UN’s 2030 Sustainable Development Agenda and Paris Agreement, and positioning the country to achieve net-zero emissions by 2050..

Key Objectives

Harness green economy opportunities

Build a green government

Smart Nation Sensor Platform (SNSP)

The Smart Nation Sensor Platform (SNSP) is a key project under Singapore's Smart Nation initiative. It involves the deployment of a nationwide network of sensors and IoT devices to collect and analyze data, which will improve the government's ability to deliver public services and enhance the quality of life for citizens.

Key Objectives

Nationwide sensor deployment

Data analytics for smart city management

Enhanced public safety and security

Lead Agencies

GovTech Singapore

Smart Nation and Digital Government Office (SNDGO)

Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA)

Tuas Mega Port Development

The Tuas Mega Port project is an ambitious initiative to develop Singapore’s next-generation port, consolidating all of Singapore’s port operations into a single, massive facility. This project will enhance Singapore's position as a leading global maritime hub and support the country's long-term economic growth.

Key Objectives

State-of-the-art port infrastructure

Automation and digitalization

Environmental sustainability measures

Green Mark Incentive Scheme for Existing Buildings (GMIS-EB)

The Green Mark Incentive Scheme for Existing Buildings (GMIS-EB) by the Building and Construction Authority (BCA) encourages building owners to improve energy efficiency and sustainability in existing buildings through retrofitting and upgrading works.

Key Benefits
Co-funding for energy efficiency upgrades
Incentives for achieving Green Mark certification
Support for sustainable building materials
Eligibility
Owners of existing buildings in Singapore
Projects must meet Green Mark criteria
Application through BCA's portal
Government of Singapore Investments
Temasek Holdings

Temasek Holdings is a global investment company headquartered in Singapore. Established in 1974, Temasek manages a net portfolio of over SGD 300 billion, with investments in a diverse range of industries including financial services, telecommunications, media, technology, transportation, industrials, consumer and real estate, life sciences, agribusiness, and energy.

Key Sectors
Technology & Telecommunications
Financial Services

Global Reach
Asia-Pacific

GIC Private Limited

GIC is a sovereign wealth fund established by the Government of Singapore in 1981. It manages Singapore’s foreign reserves with a focus on preserving and enhancing the nation’s long-term international purchasing power. GIC invests across a range of asset classes including equities, fixed income, real estate, private equity, and infrastructure.
